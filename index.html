About

I'm a rising senior at Tulane University pursuing dual degrees in Finance (Freeman School of Business) and Economics, with a minor in Philosophy. My academic path has been shaped by a genuine curiosity about how capital markets function, how companies create and destroy value, and how data-driven thinking can cut through noise.

That curiosity has taken me from a fellowship at Johns Hopkins Technology Ventures — where I worked on biopharmaceutical commercialization strategy alongside one of the nation's top tech transfer groups — to the Burkenroad Reports program, where I conducted institutional-quality equity research on Vistra Corp (NYSE: VST) and presented findings to professional investors at Tulane's annual investment conference.

I also spent a semester abroad in Madrid at ICADE, deepening my international finance perspective, and founded my own small business while in school. I'm most drawn to roles at the intersection of analytical rigor and strategic judgment — investment banking, equity research, and venture capital.

Vistra Corp — Equity Research Report

As an analyst in Tulane's Burkenroad Reports program — the nation's first university-sponsored securities analysis program — I covered Vistra Corp (NYSE: VST), one of the largest integrated retail electricity providers and power generators in the United States.

The research involved direct C-suite engagement, site visits, and a full financial model (DCF, comparable company analysis, and precedent transactions) culminating in a formal investment recommendation published in an institutional-quality report and presented at the annual Burkenroad Investment Conference to professional investors and industry participants.
